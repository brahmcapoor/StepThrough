Another bug bites the dust

With all that behind us, the fix is relatively simple: rather than setting the upper bound of the call to range to be len(lst), we set it to be len(lst) - 1. This means that the maximum possible value of i is len(lst) - 2 (or in the case of this particular program, 2). Thus, the maximum value of i + 1 is len(lst) - 1 (or in the case of this particular program, 3), which remains a valid index.

Running this modified program reveals to us the fruits of our labor: the program doesn't crash, and more to the point, gives us the correct output!
